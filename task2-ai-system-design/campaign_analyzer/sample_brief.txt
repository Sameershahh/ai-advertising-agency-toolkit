CAMPAIGN BRIEF — VERDE ORGANIC SKINCARE

Client: Verde Beauty Ltd
Campaign Name: "Return to Real"
Date: Q3 2025
Budget: $450,000 (media + production)

BACKGROUND
Verde is a premium organic skincare brand founded in 2019, sold exclusively DTC via its website
and in 120 Whole Foods locations across the US. The brand has grown 40% YoY but faces
increasing competition from mass-market "clean beauty" lines that are eroding Verde's
premium positioning.

CAMPAIGN OBJECTIVE
Reestablish Verde as the authentic authority in organic skincare and drive a 25% uplift in
repeat purchase rate among existing customers within 6 months of campaign launch.

TARGET AUDIENCE
Primary: Women aged 28–44, household income $80k+, college-educated, living in urban
and suburban markets. They are skeptical of greenwashing, research-oriented, and will
pay a premium for verified organic credentials. They follow wellness influencers but
distrust overt sponsorship.

Secondary: Millennial men aged 27–38 who have recently entered the skincare category.
Under-served by current marketing but showing 18% organic growth in purchase data.

KEY MESSAGES
1. Verde is certified USDA Organic — not "natural," not "clean" — organic.
2. Every ingredient is traceable to its farm of origin via our digital supply chain.
3. Skincare that works as hard as you do, without compromising what you stand for.

TONE OF VOICE
Confident, transparent, quietly authoritative. Not preachy. Educated but accessible.
Avoid: fear-mongering, clinical detachment, influencer-style hyperbole.

MANDATORIES
- USDA Organic seal must appear on all paid media
- Legal disclaimer required on any efficacy claims
- No before/after imagery per FDA guidelines
- Brand tagline "Real Ingredients. Real Results." must appear in all hero assets

CHANNELS
Primary: Instagram (feed + stories + reels), Pinterest
Secondary: Google Display, CTV (Hulu / Peacock pre-roll)
Consideration: Podcast sponsorships (wellness / personal finance verticals)

DELIVERABLES REQUIRED
- Hero video (30s + 15s cut-down)
- Static social suite (6 formats)
- Display banner set (5 IAB sizes)
- Email campaign (3-touch nurture sequence)

OPEN QUESTIONS / RISKS
- No confirmed talent/talent budget in brief — influencer vs. UGC approach TBD
- CTV budget allocation not confirmed — may be cut in final media plan
- Men's sub-campaign may require separate creative brief and approval process
