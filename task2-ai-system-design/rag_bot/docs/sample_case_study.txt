AGENCY CASE STUDY — VERDE BEAUTY "RETURN TO REAL" CAMPAIGN

CLIENT
Verde Beauty Ltd — premium USDA Certified Organic skincare, DTC + Whole Foods.

CHALLENGE
Verde faced commoditisation pressure from mass-market "clean beauty" entrants
eroding its premium positioning despite genuine organic certification credentials.

STRATEGY
Reframe organic certification from a product feature into a brand ethos signal.
Campaign theme: "Return to Real" — contrasting authentic, verified organic with
vague "natural" claims that dominate competitor messaging.

TARGET AUDIENCE
Primary: Women 28-44, HHI $80k+, eco-conscious, research-oriented, skeptical
of greenwashing. They read labels, check certifications, and distrust sponsored
content without disclosure.
Secondary: Millennial men 27-38 entering the skincare category for the first time.

KEY MESSAGES
1. USDA Certified Organic — not "natural," not "clean" — verified organic.
2. Every ingredient traceable to its farm of origin via digital supply chain.
3. Skincare that performs without compromising what you stand for.

RESULTS (6 months post-launch)
- Repeat purchase rate: +28% (exceeded 25% target)
- New customer acquisition cost: -14%
- Brand search volume: +42%
- Instagram engagement rate: 4.7% (industry avg: 1.1%)
- Net Promoter Score: increased from 52 to 71

CREATIVE APPROACH
Hero video (30s): Follows a single dropper bottle from an organic lavender farm
in Provence to a woman's morning routine in New York. No voice-over. Text only.
Tagline at end: "You know where it came from."

Key creative decision: No influencers in paid media. All UGC was from verified
customers with purchase history. This credibility choice drove a 3x higher
engagement rate vs. sponsored content benchmarks.

LESSONS LEARNED
1. Specificity beats aspiration: "USDA Certified Organic" outperformed "clean
   beauty" messaging in every A/B test.
2. Transparency earns trust: Farm traceability QR codes had a 22% scan rate.
3. Male sub-campaign required a separate brief and creative direction.
